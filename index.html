The Intersection of Data and Product

My core product philosophy is simple: empirically guided, human-centered. My original foundation wasn't actually in data—it was built in the trenches of Cyber Security and enterprise IT. It was when stepped into an IT Management role and taught myself SQL that my perspective shifted. I saw firsthand that data was more than just numbers on a spreadsheet; it was an engine that could unlock massive strategic possibilities through Data Science. I transitioned to Technical Product Management to own that intersection. My goal is to ensure that as we scale complex, mission-critical infrastructure, we never lose sight of the human experience on the other side of the screen.


Sustainability Strategy

I view sustainability as a core technical challenge and a distinct competitive advantage, not as a regulatory compliance checkbox. The next era of enterprise operations must factor in ecological impact alongside latency, security, and uptime. My work bridges data science and physical infrastructure—from modeling urban energy burdens and heat island effects to driving green roof feasibility studies for data centers. By systematically optimizing our compute carbon footprint and rethinking our physical assets, we can build financial systems that are as resilient for the planet as they are for the business.
